## What will I learn?

This module focuses on

1. introduction to the notion of a thread – a fundamental unit of CPU utilisation that forms the basis of multithread computer systems.
2. description of APIs for the Pthreads functions.
3. introduction to critical-section problem and impact to consistency of shared data.
4. examine several classical process-synchronisation problems such as deadlock
5. introduction on sync. tools including the mutex and Semaphore.  

This forms the core component to the subject.

## What will I be able to do at the end of this module?

At the end of this module, you should be able to answer these questions:

1. What is the multicore programming?
2. What is the difference between single and multithread processes?
3. What is the critical section in OS?
4. How to run the Pthreads function?
5. What is the critical (race) section?
6. Do you understand the “Too Much Milk Program”?
7. What are the Mutex and Semaphore? What are the difference between them?
8. What is the deadlock?
## Key vocabulary in this module:

word: Multithreads, Mutex and Semaphore, and Deadlock.
