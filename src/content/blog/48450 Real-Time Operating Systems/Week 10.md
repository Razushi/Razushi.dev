## What will I learn?

This module focuses on

1. introducing the basic concepts of real-time operating systems (RTOS)
2. explaining the applications of RTOS such as hand-held devices.
3. discussing the scheduling in RTOS such as Rate Monotonic (RM) scheduling and Early Deadline First Policy (EDF)

## What will I be able to do at the end of this module?

At the end of this module, you should be able to answer these questions:

1. 1. Why the embedded system needs a RTOS?
    2. What is the dispatch latency in RTOS?
    3. Can you give an example of missed deadlines with rate monotonic (RM) scheduling?
    4. Can you give an example of Earliest Deadline First Scheduling and why is better than RM scheduling?

## Key vocabulary in this module:

word: RTOS, rate monotonic (RM) scheduling and Earliest Deadline First Scheduling.

In this module (Real-time Operating Systems), we have learnt basic concepts of RTOS used in handheld devices. The key point to apply RTOS relays on the latency and real time CPU scheduling. For CPU scheduling in real time, two algorithms are introduced. They are RM and EDF scheduling schemes. The RM is a static algorithm while EDF is a dynamic algorithm.
