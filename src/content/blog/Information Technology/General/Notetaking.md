Imo, this is probably not my place and it's probably too late but I figure I might as well dump some info. I have extensively questioned some friends who went off deep dives in this topic, I'll run through what they taught me quickly while giving my opinions:

Obsidian, has the most flexibilty with just how many plugins it has, great customisability and pretty open community. But it does have a few potentially major problems. It's form of markdown is so janked that it doesn't even register as markdown no matter how you use pandoc. The codebase is incredibly bad, theres just no other way to describe it, also electron based so no fzf, no ripgrep, even the vim mode is a janky with the clipboard being broken, syncing with it is genuinely a 50/50, when I used it daily it seemed like every single week or so I had to make sure everything properly synced up.

Siyuan, doesn't have as many plugins as obsidian has, but it has proper exports, clean code and working sync. fortunately/unfortunately it is also cell/block based but tbh im starting to love using it. It's more obscure/hidden compared to obsidian (every other optionis tbh, their community are fanatic with promoting it) but imo it's a direct functional upgrade to Obsidian already.

Neorg, mainly for use inside of Neovim, has everything, I love it, Vhyrro has videos about it, things are moving there as well. definitely for devs and those who frequently work in neovim

Anytype, also has a claim to fame, it has multiplayer capabilities integrated with it. Bit janky last I tested about a month ago, first update cache overrides latter updates when working in the same cell, perfectly fine elsewise.

As for making use of the notes to actually make use of whole PKM schtick work, anki / srs is pretty much it. Writing down your notes is half the work, making the cards is the other half.

Imo, if your neovim dev use Neorg, in a team use Anytype, else use Siyuan. If youve been using obsidian the whole time then like check what plugins you want and compare a bit and
